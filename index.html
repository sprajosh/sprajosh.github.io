My Engineering Philosophy

Core principles that guide every system I design and every team I work with.

Speed of Iteration > Raw Speed

In 2018, I gave a talk on this exact topic. My core belief is that speed of iteration and developer time are vastly more important than raw compute speed in the early days. Optimize for shipping fast and finding product-market fit first; you can scale the architecture later.

Pragmatic Architecture

I love the bleeding edge of distributed systems, but for production, I rely on battle-tested tools. A rock-solid Postgres database and a well-structured monolith will carry a startup much further than prematurely adopting complex microservices.

Developer Experience (DevEx)

A team that isn't fighting their local environment ships faster and safer. I aggressively invest in Dockerizing projects, setting up strict CI/CD pipelines, and writing pre-commit hooks so engineers can focus on business logic, not configuration.
